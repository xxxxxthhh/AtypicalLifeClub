## 1. Business And Chain Role

Constellation's core assets are existing nuclear plants, long-term power sales, and wholesale / retail electricity-market capability. AI data centers require 24/7 reliable low-carbon power. Nuclear fits that need, but restarts, life extensions, uprates, and PPAs all depend on regulators, costs, and grid coordination.

| PJM capacity market | Data-center capacity procurement moved up to Sep 2026 | Power constraint enters market mechanism |

## 2. Bull / Bear Frame

Bull case:

1. **Nuclear is scarce 24/7 clean power for AI data centers.** Wind and solar need storage and dispatch; gas has emissions constraints; nuclear balances reliability and hyperscaler ESG needs.
2. **PPAs convert demand into contracts.** Microsoft / Meta-style long-term contracts are more verifiable than generic power-price optimism.
3. **PJM acceleration validates structural shortage.** If capacity markets price data-center load faster, CEG's existing fleet is worth more.
4. **Restart / life-extension is faster than new nuclear.** Crane restart can arrive sooner than SMRs or advanced nuclear.

Bear case:

1. **Regulatory and political risk is high.** Nuclear restart, PJM cost allocation, and residential-rate pressure can slow contract realization.
2. **Valuation is no longer cheap.** An $85B+ market cap and $107B EV embed an AI premium.
3. **If AI capex slows, the power premium retreats.** Efficiency shocks have already shown power stocks' sensitivity to compute-demand expectations.
4. **Debt and capex matter.** Nuclear life extension, restart, and safety investment require steady capital.
