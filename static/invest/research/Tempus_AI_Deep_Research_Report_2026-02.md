## 1. Company Overview

Tempus AI was founded in 2015 by serial entrepreneur Eric Lefkofsky (also co-founder of Groupon), headquartered in Chicago. The company IPO'd on NASDAQ in June 2024.

### Core Business Model: The "Data Flywheel"

Tempus operates fundamentally differently from traditional diagnostics companies:

1. **Diagnostic Testing** (revenue engine): Genomic sequencing, hereditary cancer screening, MRD testing for hospitals and physicians
2. **Data Collection** (moat builder): Every test adds de-identified clinical + molecular data to the proprietary dataset
3. **Data Licensing** (high-margin): Licensing accumulated datasets to pharma for drug development and clinical trial design
4. **AI Applications** (future growth): Building AI models on the data to assist clinical decision-making

The flywheel: **More tests → more data → better AI models → more hospitals adopt → more tests**.

### Business Mix

|---------|---------------|--------|-------|
| **Diagnostics** | ~$955M | +111% | Oncology genomics (xT/xF/xR), hereditary cancer (Ambry), MRD |

## 2. Industry Context: AI-Powered Precision Medicine

### 2.1 Market Size

- Global precision medicine: ~$85B (2025) → ~$175B (2030E), ~15% CAGR
- Genomic diagnostics: ~$25B (2025) → ~$50B (2030E)
- Healthcare AI: ~$20B (2025) → ~$70B+ (2030E)

### 2.2 Why Data Is the Core Moat

The bottleneck in precision medicine isn't algorithms — it's **data**:
- Clinical data is fragmented across thousands of hospital EMR systems
- Molecular data (genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics) requires standardized processing
- Linking clinical outcomes to molecular data requires long-term tracking
- Data access is strictly regulated (HIPAA)

Tempus collects first-party data directly through its diagnostics business, bypassing traditional data aggregation fragmentation.

### 2.3 Competitive Landscape

| Company | Position | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Tempus AI** | Full-stack precision medicine platform | Data flywheel, multimodal data, AI capability | Still unprofitable, high valuation |
| **Foundation Medicine** (Roche) | Comprehensive genomic profiling | Roche resources, multiple FDA approvals | Innovation slowed post-acquisition |
| **Guardant Health** | Liquid biopsy | Blood-based testing leadership | Narrow data dimensions |
| **Natera** | MRD/NIPT | MRD testing leader, strong reimbursement | Small data licensing business |
| **Exact Sciences** | Cancer screening | Cologuard brand, large-scale commercialization | Weak AI capabilities |
| **Illumina** | Sequencing platform | Sequencing-instrument monopoly | Limited downstream applications |

**Tempus's differentiation:** The only company simultaneously operating large-scale diagnostics + data licensing + AI applications as a full-stack platform. Competitors either only run diagnostics (Guardant, Natera) or only operate a platform (Flatiron/Roche) — no one else strings the entire chain together the way Tempus does.

## 4. Data-Moat Deep Assessment

### 4.1 Data Assets

Tempus claims the world's largest clinical + molecular multimodal dataset:
- Genomic data: millions of sequencing results
- Clinical data: deep integration with EMR systems
- Pathology data: 7M digitized pathology slides (Paige acquisition)
- Patient records: 30M+ (Deep 6 AI)
- Outcomes data: long-term tracking of treatment outcomes

### 4.2 Moat Strength

**Strong:**
- Data carries legal barriers (HIPAA compliance; competitors cannot legally access it)
- EMR integration with hospital systems is high-stickiness (high switching cost)
- 126% net revenue retention proves customer stickiness
- The larger the dataset, the better the AI models — a positive feedback loop

**Weakening factors:**
- Foundation Medicine (Roche) also holds large volumes of genomic data
- Flatiron Health (Roche) is competitive in the clinical-data domain
- Large hospital systems may build their own data platforms
- Data-licensing revenue growth (FY2025 31%) still trails diagnostics, though Insights accelerated to +44.1% in Q1 2026

### 4.3 SoftBank Japan JV

In June 2024, Tempus formed the "SB TEMPUS" joint venture with SoftBank to bring its data + AI platform to the Japanese market. This is an important step in international expansion, but its revenue contribution is currently minimal.

## 7. Investment Thesis: Bull vs. Bear

### 🐂 Bull Case

**1. Data flywheel moat (moderate-high conviction)**
Tempus's data assets carry legal barriers and network effects. Every test reinforces the moat. The 126% net revenue retention proves exceptionally strong customer stickiness. This is not an ordinary SaaS company but a platform holding non-replicable data assets.

**2. Precision medicine megatrend (high conviction)**
AI + genomics transforming healthcare is a high-certainty secular trend. Tempus sits at the core of it. The global precision-medicine market doubles by 2030.

**3. Topline re-accelerating (moderate conviction)**
Q1 2026 revenue grew +36.1% — now largely organic as Ambry laps — an acceleration versus the FY2025 ~33.5% organic rate, and management raised full-year guidance to $1.59-1.60B. Data & Apps (Insights +44.1%) led, exactly the high-margin engine the thesis depends on.

**4. MRD explosion (moderate conviction)**
MRD volume reached ~6,500 tests in Q1 2026, up ~500% YoY; FDA recognition of MRD as a clinical endpoint will drive further demand. Medicare reimbursement, once in place, would be a major catalyst.

**5. Acquisition integration (moderate conviction)**
The Ambry and Paige acquisitions expand data dimensions (hereditary cancer + pathology) and strengthen the flywheel; a new Merck collaboration signals external validation of the data-licensing business.

### 🐻 Bear Case

**1. Growth quality and durability (moderate conviction)**
FY2025's 83% headline was acquisition-aided; Q1 2026 +36% is healthier (mostly organic), but full-year guidance still implies deceleration to ~25-26%, so the Q1 pace has to prove durable rather than a comp-driven peak.

**2. GAAP losses widening (fact)**
Q1 2026 GAAP EPS -$0.71 and net loss -$125.9M — nearly double the -$68.0M of Q1'25. Adjusted-EBITDA positivity is a guidance target, not GAAP profit; SBC and acquisition amortization are excluded.

**3. CEO persistent selling (fact)**
Eric Lefkofsky has sold $127M+ in stock cumulatively, never buying. Under the dual-class structure, minority shareholders cannot push back. This is the biggest governance red flag.

**4. Extremely high short interest (fact)**
20.6% short interest means significant capital is betting on a decline. High short interest is both a risk (a short report could surface at any time) and a potential catalyst (a short squeeze).

**5. Valuation not cheap (moderate-high conviction)**
P/S 7x, EV/2026E EBITDA ~150x for a still-unprofitable company with widening GAAP losses — the valuation demands continued near-perfect execution.

**6. Data licensing scale vs diagnostics (moderate conviction)**
Data & Apps accelerated to +40.5% in Q1, but it is still only ~25% of revenue; if the high-margin business can't keep outgrowing diagnostics, margin-expansion room is limited.

### Bottom Line

Tempus AI is precision medicine's most ambitious "full-stack" player — diagnostics + data + AI in a unique flywheel that is one of a kind in the industry. Q1 2026 revenue of $348.1M (+36.1%), 126% net retention, and a raised full-year outlook prove strong, still-accelerating product-market fit.

But the other side is equally prominent: the GAAP net loss nearly doubled YoY to $(125.9)M in Q1, full-year guidance still implies deceleration to ~25-26%, the CEO has sold $127M+ without a single purchase, and 20%+ short interest signals significant bearish conviction. P/S 7x still demands the company execute near-flawlessly while bending the loss curve.

**Risk-matching characteristics (descriptive, not investment advice):**
- Volatility: 20%+ short interest implies high volatility, calling for a risk budget appropriate to a high-volatility, high-beta asset
- Time horizon: GAAP profitability may remain 2-3 years out, fitting a long-term lens rather than a near-term certainty mandate
- Governance: dual-class structure + persistent CEO selling are structural features to factor in beforehand
- Valuation sensitivity: P/S 7x / EV-EBITDA ~150x is highly sensitive to the profitability inflection; any growth slowdown or loss-widening is amplified
- Drawdown: the trailing 52-week range shows 30-50% drawdowns are possible and are better treated as a base case than a tail

**Key watch point:** The 2026 Q2 earnings are the next critical window — whether the +36% pace holds and the GAAP loss curve bends. If growth stays in the 30s% and EBITDA tracks toward the ~$65M target, the valuation has support; if growth decelerates toward guidance or losses keep widening, the short side may intensify its attack.
