## 1. Business Overview

### What Is Salesforce

Founded in 1999, Salesforce is the world's largest CRM software company and a pioneer of the SaaS model. It commands ~21-26% of the global CRM market ‚Äî 4-5x the share of second-place Microsoft Dynamics. Added to the Dow Jones Industrial Average in 2024.

### How It Makes Money

Nearly all revenue (~95%) comes from subscriptions, across five cloud products:

**Five Core Clouds:**
- Sales Cloud (sales automation) ‚Äî largest revenue contributor
- Service Cloud (customer service) ‚Äî second largest
- Platform & Other (Data Cloud, Heroku, MuleSoft)
- Marketing & Commerce Cloud
- Tableau (analytics)

**New Growth Engines:**
- Agentforce (AI Agent platform) ‚Äî ~~ARR $540M, +330% YoY~~ ARR $800M (+169% YoY, Q4 FY2026)

### CRM Market Overview

Global CRM market ~$100-113B in 2025, projected to reach ~$263B by 2032 (CAGR ~13%). AI in CRM market worth ~$11B in 2025, expected to reach $48.4B by 2033.

|-----------|-----------------|----------------|---------------------|

| Oracle/SAP | ~3-5% each | ERP integration | Low-Medium |

### Moat Assessment

**üè∞ Strong Moat Factors:**

1. **Extremely High Switching Costs**
   - CRM is a core enterprise system; migration costs are enormous
   - Average customer lifecycle >10 years
   - Deep customization makes replacement nearly impossible
   - 50%+ of Fortune 500 use Data Cloud

2. **Ecosystem Network Effects**
   - AppExchange has 7,000+ third-party apps
   - Millions of certified Salesforce developers/admins globally
   - Massive partner ecosystem (Accenture, Deloitte, etc.)

3. **Brand & Market Position**
   - 21-26% CRM market share, 4-5x the second place
   - Dow Jones Industrial Average constituent
   - "CRM" is literally Salesforce's ticker ‚Äî brand equals category

**üèöÔ∏è Weak Moat Factors:**

1. **Slowing Growth**
   - Revenue growth decelerated from +24% (FY22) to +9% (FY25)
   - Growth ceiling for large enterprise software becoming visible
   - Whether AI can re-accelerate growth remains uncertain

2. **Pricing Pressure**
   - 2025's 6% price hike drew customer pushback
   - Low-cost alternatives like HubSpot eroding SMB share
   - Extra charges for AI features may face resistance

**Overall Moat Rating: Strong (üè∞)**
Salesforce's moat derives primarily from extremely high switching costs and ecosystem lock-in ‚Äî among the strongest in enterprise software.

## 6. üêª Bear Case (Steel-Manned)

**Core Thesis:** Salesforce is a maturing SaaS company with decelerating growth. The AI narrative masks the reality of a weakening core business. Declining net retention and intensifying competition mean "margin expansion" may just be another way of saying "growth has peaked."

**Supporting Evidence:**

**1. Growth Engine Stalling**
- ~~Revenue growth from +24% ‚Üí +9%, persistent deceleration~~ Revenue growth stabilized at +10% (FY26), but FY27 guidance of +10-11% shows no meaningful acceleration despite massive AI investment
- ~~Net retention rate dropped to ~100% ‚Äî existing customers no longer expanding, the most dangerous signal~~ Revenue attrition ~8% ‚Äî existing customers still not expanding meaningfully
- Core CRM market approaching saturation, rising customer acquisition costs
- 6% price hike-driven growth is unsustainable and may accelerate churn

**2. AI Monetization May Be a Mirage**
- ~~Agentforce ARR $540M is only 1.3% of total revenue ‚Äî far from "game-changing"~~ Agentforce standalone ARR $800M is still only ~1.9% of revenue; the $2.9B combined figure includes Data 360 which is partly legacy Data Cloud rebranded
- Consumption-based pricing means unpredictable revenue, and customers may use very little
- Enterprise AI procurement cycles are long; POC to full deployment can take 2-3 years
- Microsoft Copilot + Dynamics bundling strategy may be more compelling
- FY27 guidance of only +10-11% despite $2.9B AI ARR suggests AI is cannibalizing, not adding to, core revenue

**3. Competitive Landscape Deteriorating**
- Microsoft Dynamics growing at 23%, far faster than Salesforce's 9%
- HubSpot continuously eroding SMB market share
- ServiceNow's backend workflows + AI creating cross-boundary competition
- Open-source CRM and vertical SaaS fragmenting niche markets

**4. Informatica Acquisition Risks**
- $8B price tag is not cheap, integration complexity is high
- Salesforce's large acquisition integration track record is mixed (Slack ROI still debated)
- Data management market is fiercely competitive (Databricks, Snowflake, etc.)
- Integration may distract management attention

**Downside Scenario:** Revenue growth drops to 6-7%, AI monetization disappoints, net retention continues declining, Forward P/E compresses to 15x, stock could fall to $150-170.
