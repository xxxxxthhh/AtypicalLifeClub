## Executive Summary

Synopsys is the EDA/IP common-constraint report for P2. If AI silicon demand is real, it should not only show up in foundry capex and semicap tools; it should also show up in design automation, verification, simulation, IP blocks, and system-level complexity. SNPS tests the design-start and complexity side of the chain.

Q2 FY2026 revenue was $2.276B, above guidance, versus $1.604B in Q2 FY2025. GAAP EPS was $0.09 and non-GAAP EPS was $3.35. Management guided FY2026 revenue to $9.625-9.705B, non-GAAP EPS to $14.72-14.80, operating cash flow to about $2.3B, free cash flow to about $2.0B, and capex to about $300M. FY2026 revenue guidance includes about $2.96B of expected Ansys revenue, including about $60M of channel / accounting impact.

At the 2026-07-02 close of $437.16, SNPS had about $83.7B market cap and $92.1B EV. Against the $9.665B FY2026 revenue midpoint, EV / sales is about 9.5x. Against the $14.76 non-GAAP EPS midpoint, P/E is about 29.6x.

## 1. Business And Chain Role

SNPS links AI compute architecture to the design infrastructure needed to build it. Design Automation includes silicon design, verification, system integration, digital / custom / FPGA IC design software, manufacturing software, and Ansys products. Design IP includes logic libraries, embedded memories, wired and memory interface IP, security IP, and embedded processors.

This is why Synopsys belongs beside semicap equipment in P2. Tool orders tell us whether fabs are building capacity; EDA and IP tell us whether customers are starting, verifying, simulating, and taping out increasingly complex chips and systems.

## 2. Bull / Bear Frame

**Bull case:** AI scaling increases chip architectural diversity and system complexity. That expands verification, simulation, IP, and multiphysics demand. The Ansys acquisition broadens SNPS from chip design into system simulation, raising the chance that AI infrastructure complexity creates a larger software constraint.

**Bear case:** Q2 GAAP EPS was only $0.09 because acquisition and integration effects are heavy. The Ansys integration is large, revenue guidance includes acquired revenue, and the company explicitly assumes no further export-control or Entity List changes. Any integration drag or China/export tightening would weaken the design-start read-through.

## 3. Key Monitoring

1. **Ansys integration:** FY2026 guidance includes about $2.96B of Ansys revenue. Watch whether integration expands the platform or dilutes execution.
2. **Design Automation growth:** the core signal is whether AI design complexity drives verification, manufacturing software, and simulation demand.
3. **Design IP pull-through:** memory interface IP, embedded memories, security IP, and processors should benefit if custom AI silicon broadens.
4. **Export-control assumption:** guidance assumes no further export-control / Entity List changes. That is a live risk.
5. **FCF conversion:** FY2026 free cash flow guide is about $2.0B. Cash conversion should recover as integration noise normalizes.

## 4. Valuation Context

Current price-ledger input: $437.16 close on 2026-07-02, market cap about $83.7B, EV about $92.1B. FY2026 revenue midpoint is $9.665B and non-GAAP EPS midpoint is $14.76, implying about 9.5x EV / sales and about 29.6x non-GAAP P/E.

SNPS is cheaper than CDNS on forward sales and earnings because Ansys integration and GAAP noise create more uncertainty. The valuation is reasonable only if Ansys expands the addressable design / simulation stack and if export controls do not disrupt the guide.

## 5. Conclusion

SNPS is the EDA/IP common constraint. It is the design-side counterpart to ASML's manufacturing bottleneck. The stance is **neutral-watch**: AI complexity supports the long-term thesis, but Ansys integration, export risk, and acquired-revenue quality need close monitoring.
