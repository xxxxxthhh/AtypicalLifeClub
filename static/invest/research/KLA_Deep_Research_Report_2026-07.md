## Executive Summary

KLA is the process-control and yield dashboard for the semicap-equipment layer. AI capacity is not only a question of installing lithography, etch, and deposition tools; advanced nodes, HBM, advanced packaging, and high-density interconnects also need yield learning, inspection, metrology, and process-control intensity. KLAC tests whether fabs are paying for that control layer.

Fiscal Q3 2026 revenue was $3.416B, above the $3.35B guidance midpoint. GAAP diluted EPS was $9.12 and non-GAAP EPS was $9.40 on the company's as-reported share basis. Operating cash flow was $707.5M for the quarter and $4.40B over the last twelve months; free cash flow was $622.3M for the quarter and $4.01B over the last twelve months. Semiconductor Process Control revenue was $3.084B, the dominant segment.

At the 2026-07-02 close of $235.55, current market data showed about $307.7B market cap and $308.9B EV. Because current price and share data appear split-adjusted while the Q3 EPS disclosure is as reported for that quarter, this report avoids a direct P/E comparison and uses EV / revenue and FCF yield instead. EV / Q3 annualized revenue is about 22.6x, and LTM FCF yield is about 1.3%.

## 1. Business And Chain Role

KLA sits where AI capex turns into manufacturable yield. In the P2 map, ASML answers "can customers get lithography capacity?", AMAT and LRCX answer "is process-tool demand broad?", and KLA answers "are fabs investing in the control layer needed to make advanced devices reliably?"

This matters because AI chips are increasingly constrained by packaging, memory bandwidth, interconnect density, and node complexity. A fab can announce a leading-edge line, but without process control and yield learning, capacity is not economically useful.

## 2. Bull / Bear Frame

**Bull case:** KLA's Q3 print showed record-level process-control demand, strong cash generation, and management confidence in calendar 2026. The company explicitly linked demand to AI ecosystem growth across foundry / logic, memory, advanced packaging, and services. The board also approved a $7B additional repurchase authorization and a $2.30 quarterly dividend.

**Bear case:** the stock already prices process control as a scarce, high-quality toll booth. EV / annualized revenue above 20x and low FCF yield leave little room for a WFE pause. If customers digest capacity, delay advanced-node ramps, or slow memory capex, KLA's quality will not fully protect the multiple.

## 3. Key Monitoring

1. **Semiconductor Process Control revenue:** Q3 revenue was $3.084B, far larger than Specialty Semiconductor Process or PCB / Component Inspection.
2. **AI growth vectors:** management called out foundry / logic, memory, advanced packaging, and services. Watch whether all remain firm.
3. **FCF and capital return:** LTM FCF was $4.01B and LTM capital returns were $3.15B. Buybacks are helpful only if cycle risk stays controlled.
4. **Segment breadth:** Specialty Semiconductor Process was $164M and PCB / Component Inspection was $167.6M; these provide smaller but useful cross-checks.
5. **Calendar-2026 outlook:** any guidance softening would challenge the process-control scarcity premium.

## 4. Valuation Context

Current price-ledger input: $235.55 close on 2026-07-02, market cap about $307.7B, EV about $308.9B. Q3 revenue annualized is about $13.66B, implying roughly 22.6x EV / annualized revenue. LTM FCF of $4.01B implies about a 1.3% FCF yield on market cap.

This is the highest-quality but also the most multiple-sensitive semicap dashboard in P2. If process-control intensity keeps rising with AI manufacturing complexity, the premium can persist. If WFE orders cool, KLAC may be right fundamentally and still wrong at the current price.

## 5. Conclusion

KLAC is a necessary P2 report because it tests whether AI capacity is not just announced, but yieldable. The stance is **neutral-watch**: the business quality is clear, while the current valuation demands continued process-control scarcity and very strong cash conversion.
