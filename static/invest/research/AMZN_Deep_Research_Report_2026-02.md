## I. Business Overview & Competitive Landscape

### 1.1 Business Architecture

Amazon's business can be understood along two dimensions:

**By Reporting Segment (3):**
- **North America:** FY2025 revenue $426.3B (59%) — U.S. and Canada e-commerce, physical stores, advertising, subscriptions
- **International:** FY2025 revenue $161.9B (23%) — all other global markets
- **AWS:** FY2025 revenue $128.7B (18%) — cloud computing and AI services

**By Revenue Stream (7):**

| Revenue Stream | FY2025 | YoY Growth | Share |
|---------------|--------|------------|-------|
| Online Stores | $269.3B | ~8% | 37.6% |
| Third-Party Seller Services | $172.2B | ~10% | 24.0% |
| AWS | $128.7B | +20% | 18.0% |
| Advertising Services | $68.6B | +22% | 9.6% |
| Subscription Services | $49.6B | +12% | 6.9% |
| Physical Stores | $22.6B | +6% | 3.1% |
| Other | $6.0B | +8% | 0.8% |

### 1.2 Flywheel Effects

Amazon's core competitive advantage lies in its self-reinforcing flywheels:

**Retail Flywheel:** More sellers → more selection → lower prices → more buyers → more sellers. Third-party sellers accounted for 61-62% of paid units in FY2025, deepening platform effects.

**AWS Flywheel:** Scale → lower costs → lower prices → more customers → greater scale. AWS operates the world's largest cloud infrastructure, with scale advantages that are extremely difficult to replicate.

**Advertising Flywheel:** More traffic → higher ad value → more ad revenue → invest in user experience → more traffic. Advertising has become one of Amazon's highest-margin businesses.

**AI Flywheel (Emerging):** Custom chips lower costs → more competitive AI services → more customers → more data and scale → better AI models and services.

### 1.3 Competitive Landscape

**Cloud Computing (AWS vs Azure vs GCP):**
- AWS remains the world's largest cloud provider, FY2025 revenue $128.7B, Q4 growth accelerated to 24% (fastest in 13 quarters)
- Microsoft Azure growing ~30%+, leveraging OpenAI partnership for enterprise AI advantage
- Google Cloud growing ~28-30%, differentiated in AI/ML native tooling
- AWS advantages: broadest service portfolio, largest customer base, custom chips (Trainium/Graviton) for cost reduction

**E-Commerce Retail:**
- ~37-40% U.S. e-commerce market share, far ahead of #2
- Key competition: Walmart (omnichannel acceleration), Shopify (empowering independent brands), Temu/Shein (ultra-low price)
- Amazon's response: Amazon Haul (1M+ items under $10), expanded Same-Day Delivery coverage

**Advertising:**
- Now the third-largest U.S. digital ad platform (behind Google and Meta)
- FY2025 ad revenue $68.6B, growing 22%, well above industry average
- Core advantage: purchase intent data (users searching on Amazon have buying intent)
- Prime Video ads are a new growth engine (TNF averaging 15M+ viewers, NBA debut)

## II. AWS Deep Dive

### 2.1 Growth Re-Acceleration

AWS demonstrated impressive growth acceleration throughout FY2025:

| Quarter | Revenue | YoY Growth | Operating Income | Op. Margin |
|---------|---------|------------|-----------------|------------|
| Q1 2025 | $29.3B | +17% | $11.5B | 39.5% |

| Q3 2025 | $33.0B | +20% | $11.4B | 34.6% |
| Q4 2025 | $35.6B | +24% | $12.5B | 35.0% |
| **FY2025** | **$128.7B** | **+20%** | **$45.6B** | **35.4%** |

Q4's 24% growth was the fastest in 13 quarters, signaling that AI demand is materially driving AWS growth.

### 2.2 AI Strategy & Custom Silicon

Amazon is pursuing a "full-stack" AI strategy:

**Custom Chip Ecosystem:**
- **Trainium + Graviton annualized revenue exceeds $10B**, growing at triple-digit rates YoY
- **Trainium2:** Fully subscribed, 1.4M chips deployed, powers majority of inference on Bedrock (used by 100K+ companies)
- **Trainium3:** Now delivering production workloads, nearly all supply expected committed by mid-2026
- **Trainium4:** Expected 2027, 6x FP4 compute, 4x memory bandwidth vs Trainium3
- **Graviton5:** Latest-gen general-purpose CPU, used by 90%+ of top 1,000 AWS customers, up to 40% more price-performant than x86

**AI Services Layer:**
- **Amazon Bedrock:** 20+ managed models (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, NVIDIA, etc.), 100K+ enterprise customers
- **Nova Model Family:** Nova 2 Lite/Pro, Nova 2 Sonic, Nova Forge (custom pre-training), Nova Act (UI agent automation)
- **Bedrock AgentCore:** Agent infrastructure (Policy, Evaluations, Memory)
- **Kiro Agent:** Autonomous bug triage, code coverage tasks

**Major Customer Wins:** OpenAI, Visa, NBA, BlackRock, Perplexity, Lyft, United Airlines, DoorDash, Salesforce, U.S. Air Force, Adobe, Thomson Reuters, AT&T, HSBC, and more.

### 2.3 AWS Margin Analysis

AWS FY2025 operating margin was 35.4%, down from 37.0% in FY2024. This reflects:

**Margin Headwinds:**
- Massive AI infrastructure buildout (data centers, chips, power)
- Low utilization rates on newly launched services
- Competitive pricing pressure from Azure and GCP

**Margin Tailwinds:**
- Custom silicon reducing unit costs (Graviton is 40% more price-performant than x86)
- Continued scale economies
- Premium pricing on high-value AI services (Bedrock, SageMaker)

**Key Judgment:** AWS margin compression is a deliberate choice — Amazon is trading margin for AI-era market share and ecosystem lock-in. Long-term, custom chip cost advantages and high-value AI services should support margin recovery.

## V. Financial Deep Dive

### 5.1 Revenue Mix Shift

Amazon's revenue structure is shifting from low-margin product sales to high-margin services:

| Category | FY2025 | FY2024 | Change |

| Product Sales | $296.3B (41%) | $272.3B (43%) | +8.8% |
| Service Sales | $420.7B (59%) | $365.6B (57%) | +15.1% |

Services revenue (AWS, advertising, 3P seller services, subscriptions) is growing significantly faster than product sales and carries higher margins. This structural shift is the core driver of Amazon's ongoing margin improvement.

### 5.2 Margin Trends

| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|

Sustained margin improvement reflects: operational efficiency gains, increasing share of high-margin businesses (advertising, AWS), and cost optimization following the 2022-2023 over-expansion period.

### 5.3 Capital Expenditure: The $200B Bet

This is the central question for understanding Amazon's current investment thesis.

| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Capex | $131.8B | $83.0B | $52.7B | $63.6B |
| Operating Cash Flow | $139.5B | $115.9B | $84.9B | $46.8B |
| Free Cash Flow (TTM) | $11.2B | $38.2B | $32.2B | -$16.9B |
| Capex/Revenue | 18.4% | 13.0% | 9.2% | 12.4% |

**FY2025 capex of $131.8B was up 59% YoY.** Andy Jassy explicitly stated plans to invest approximately $200B in 2026, primarily for AI infrastructure.

**Where the capex is going:**
- AI data center construction and expansion
- Custom chip (Trainium, Graviton) production and deployment
- Project Rainier (world's largest AI compute cluster, 500K+ Trainium2 chips, used by Anthropic to train Claude)
- Low Earth Orbit satellite (Leo/Kuiper) infrastructure
- Continued logistics network optimization

**FCF Impact:** Free cash flow plunged from $38.2B in FY2024 to $11.2B, a 71% decline. This is a deliberate choice — trading near-term cash flow for long-term AI infrastructure advantage.

## VII. Bull Case

### 7.1 Winner-Take-Most in AI Infrastructure

**Core Thesis:** Amazon is building the most complete AI infrastructure stack — from custom silicon to cloud services to end-user applications. This vertical integration capability is unmatched in the industry.

- Trainium + Graviton annualized revenue exceeding $10B with triple-digit growth proves the custom chip strategy is paying off
- AWS Q4 growth accelerated to 24% (fastest in 13 quarters), with AI demand as the core driver
- Bedrock platform aggregating 20+ models for 100K+ enterprise customers creates an "app store" effect for AI services
- $200B capex looks aggressive, but if AI is truly a once-in-a-generation technology shift, first-mover infrastructure investment builds insurmountable scale barriers

### 7.2 Hidden Value in Advertising

- $68.6B ad revenue growing at 22%, with estimated 50%+ margins
- If spun off, advertising alone would be worth $500B+
- Prime Video advertising is just beginning; streaming ads are a major growth market over the next 3-5 years
- AI-driven personalized advertising will further improve conversion rates and ARPU

### 7.3 Structural Retail Margin Improvement

- Gross margin improved from 43.8% in FY2022 to 50.3% in FY2025 — clear trend
- Robotics and automation continue reducing logistics costs
- Rising services mix (59% vs 57%) drives blended margin improvement
- International segment now consistently profitable, with significant margin expansion runway

## VIII. Bear Case

### 8.1 Capex Overinvestment Risk

**Core Thesis:** The $200B 2026 capex guidance is a massive bet. If AI demand disappoints or the competitive landscape shifts, Amazon faces significant asset impairments and declining ROIC.

- FY2025 FCF already collapsed from $38.2B to $11.2B (-71%); FY2026 could deteriorate further
- Historical precedent: Amazon's 2022 logistics over-expansion crushed margins and halved the stock price
- AI infrastructure investment has long payback periods (data centers last 15-20 years), but AI technology may iterate every 2-3 years
- If AI inference costs continue falling rapidly (DeepSeek effect), returns on massive infrastructure investment may disappoint

### 8.2 Intensifying AWS Competition

- Microsoft Azure leveraging exclusive OpenAI partnership for enterprise AI market advantage
- Google Cloud differentiated in AI/ML native tooling
- AWS operating margin already declined from 39.5% in Q1 to 35.0% in Q4 — competitive pressure visible
- Enterprise multi-cloud strategies reduce AWS lock-in effects

### 8.3 Structural Retail Challenges

- E-commerce growth slowing to single digits (Online Stores +8%), approaching industry average
- Temu/Shein disruption in low-price segment (Amazon Haul is a defensive response)
- Physical stores ($22.6B) growing slowly (+6%), Whole Foods transformation showing limited results
- Shipping costs continue rising: FY2025 worldwide shipping costs $102.7B

### 8.4 Regulatory & Macro Risks

- Antitrust scrutiny: FTC lawsuit still ongoing
- Tariff policy uncertainty: Q1 2026 guidance explicitly mentions tariff and trade policy risks
- Macro slowdown could impact consumer spending and enterprise IT budgets
- Q4 special charges of $2.4B (Italy tax disputes, severance, asset impairments) hint at operational complexity

### Investment Recommendation

**Stance: Cautiously Bullish**

Amazon is one of the few companies with dual moats in both consumer internet and enterprise cloud computing. AWS re-accelerating to 24% growth, advertising at 22%, and retail margins steadily improving — these are real fundamental improvements.

The $200B 2026 capex guidance is the biggest point of debate. We lean toward viewing it as the right strategic choice — in AI infrastructure, scale and first-mover advantage are critical, and Amazon has sufficient cash flow and balance sheet strength to support this investment. However, we acknowledge that if AI demand disappoints or technology trajectories shift materially, returns on this investment could disappoint.

**Key Monitoring Metrics:**
- Whether AWS quarterly growth sustains 20%+
- Whether advertising revenue growth sustains 20%+
- Custom chip revenue growth trajectory
- Whether FCF begins recovering in H2 FY2026
- Whether Q1 2026 operating income reaches guidance midpoint or above
