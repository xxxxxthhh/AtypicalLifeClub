## 1. Company Overview

SK hynix is the world's second-largest memory chipmaker (behind Samsung Electronics) and the dominant leader in HBM. Founded in 1983, headquartered in Icheon, South Korea. Parent company SK Square holds ~20% equity.

### Business Mix

| Segment | Revenue Share | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| **DRAM** | ~73% | Including HBM, DDR5, LPDDR5 |
| **NAND** | ~27% | Rose to global #2 after acquiring Intel's NAND business in 2021 |

### HBM: The Profit Engine

HBM is high-performance memory designed for AI accelerators (GPUs/TPUs), achieving extreme bandwidth through vertical stacking of DRAM dies.

- HBM ASP is 5-6x conventional DRAM
- HBM margins estimated at 60-70%+
- Growing share of DRAM revenue, now the core profit driver

## 4. HBM Technology Roadmap & Competition

### 4.1 Technology Evolution

| Generation | Stack | Bandwidth | Mass Production | SK hynix Position |
|-----------|-------|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| HBM3 | 8-layer | 819 GB/s | 2023 | Leader |
| HBM3e | 12-layer | 1.2 TB/s | 2024-2025 | NVIDIA exclusive/primary |
| HBM4 | 12-16 layer | 2.8 TB/s | 2026 H2 | Competition intensifying |

### 4.2 HBM4: The Competitive Inflection

- All three (SK hynix, Samsung, Micron) have submitted 16-Hi HBM4 samples to NVIDIA
- Samsung reports 11.7 Gbps/pin test speed (SK hynix targets 10 Gbps)
- NVIDIA Rubin GPU will be the first HBM4 customer
- SK hynix's exclusive advantage may narrow in the HBM4 era

### 4.3 Moat Assessment

**Strong:** Advanced packaging (TSV, hybrid bonding) 12-18 month lead; deep NVIDIA co-development; yield advantage; 2026 capacity sold out.

**Weakening:** Samsung HBM4 catch-up accelerating; Micron gaining U.S. policy support; HBM4 architecture changes may reset competition.

## 6. Bull vs. Bear Case

### 🐂 Bull Case

1. **HBM monopoly** — 53% share + NVIDIA lock-in. 2026 capacity sold out.
2. **Extreme undervaluation** — Forward P/E 5.3x for 49% margins and 100%+ growth.
3. **AI capex supercycle** — $300B+ hyperscaler capex in 2026. HBM is the highest-certainty AI beneficiary.
4. **Technology lead** — 12-18 month yield advantage over Samsung in advanced packaging.
5. **Improving shareholder returns** — Record FY2025 dividends, Korea Value-up program.

### 🐻 Bear Case

1. **Memory cyclicality** — Industry cycles every 3-4 years. FY2023 was a massive loss year.
2. **HBM4 competition** — Samsung catching up, exclusive premium may shrink.
3. **HBM pricing pressure** — 10-15% price decline possible in H2 2026 as supply increases.
4. **Customer concentration** — Heavy NVIDIA dependence.
5. **Korea Discount** — Chaebol governance, minority shareholder protection concerns.
6. **Geopolitics** — Korean semiconductors caught in US-China tensions.
