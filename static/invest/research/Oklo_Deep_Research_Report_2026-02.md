## 1. Business Overview

### What Oklo Does

Oklo Inc. is an advanced nuclear technology company developing small modular reactors (SMRs), specifically fast-neutron microreactors called "Aurora powerhouses." The company operates under a unique business model: rather than selling reactors, Oklo plans to own, operate, and maintain the powerhouses while selling electricity directly to customers through long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs).

**Core Business Lines:**

1. **Aurora Powerhouse Development** (Primary)
   - Liquid-metal-cooled, metal-fueled fast reactors
   - Capacity range: 15-75 MW (with plans for 100+ MW designs)
   - Initial design: 1.5 MWe microreactor, now expanded to larger configurations
   - Target market: Data centers, industrial facilities, remote locations
   - 20-year operational life before refueling (sealed core replacement)

2. **Nuclear Fuel Recycling** (Strategic)
   - Acquired Atomic Alchemy in 2024
   - Developing first privately-funded fuel recycling facility in Oak Ridge, Tennessee
   - Investment: Up to $1.68 billion
   - Technology: Electrorefining-based pyroprocessing
   - Goal: Extract >90% of remaining energy from used nuclear fuel
   - Expected production: Early 2030s
   - Additional revenue stream: Radioisotope production

### How They Make Money (Planned)

**Revenue Model:** Power-as-a-Service
- Sell electricity at contracted rates ($/MWh) through long-term PPAs
- Own and operate the powerhouses (capital-intensive model)
- Customers avoid upfront capital costs and operational complexity
- Oklo retains asset ownership and long-term revenue stream

**Key Segments:**
- **Data Centers:** Primary focus given AI/computing power demands
- **Industrial Applications:** Manufacturing, chemical plants
- **Remote/Off-grid:** Mining operations, military bases
- **Future:** Fuel recycling services and radioisotope sales

### Technology Differentiation

**Aurora Fast Reactor Technology:**
- **Passive Safety:** Self-stabilizing, self-controlling, "walk-away safe"
- **Natural Cooling:** No active cooling systems required
- **HALEU Fuel:** High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium (5-20% enrichment)
- **Fast Neutron Spectrum:** Enables fuel recycling and waste reduction
- **Modular Design:** Factory fabrication potential for cost reduction
- **Heritage:** Based on 400+ reactor-years of fast reactor operating experience (including EBR-II)

**2. Economics are Unproven and Likely Unfavorable**

SMR economics have consistently disappointed:
- **NuScale precedent:** LCOE estimates rose from $58/MWh to $89/MWh due to cost overruns

- **Unproven cost structure:** No operating reactor to validate economics
- **Capital intensity:** Must fund construction of every powerhouse
- **Scale disadvantage:** Smaller reactors (15-75 MW) have worse economics than large nuclear
- **Manufacturing unproven:** Factory fabrication benefits require volume not yet achieved

**Reality check:** If first Aurora costs $500M to build (not unreasonable for FOAK) and generates 50 MW at 85% capacity factor, that's 372 GWh/year. At $80/MWh, revenue is $30M/year. **Payback period: 17 years before considering operating costs.**

The fuel supply chain does not exist at scale:
- **Current domestic capacity:** <1 metric ton/year
- **Projected demand:** 10 MT/year currently, 50 MT/year by 2035
- **Supply gap:** 10x shortage
- **Geopolitical risk:** Russia is primary HALEU supplier globally
- **Timeline risk:** Domestic production ramping but not proven at scale
- **Oklo's recycling facility:** Not operational until early 2030s

**Implication:** Even if NRC approves Aurora, Oklo may not be able to fuel it at scale for years.

The 14 GW "pipeline" is marketing, not reality:
- **Switch 12 GW:** Non-binding Master Power Agreement through 2044
- **Meta 1.2 GW:** Letter of intent, not binding contract
- **Others 750 MWe:** Letters of intent
- **Conversion risk:** Zero binding PPAs signed
- **Customer optionality:** Data centers can choose grid power, renewables, or other SMRs
- **Pricing uncertainty:** No disclosed pricing terms in public agreements

**Historical precedent:** Non-binding agreements in capital-intensive industries frequently fail to convert. Customers face no penalty for walking away.

**5. Capital Requirements Will Force Massive Dilution**

The math doesn't work with current cash:
- **Current cash:** $1.2B
- **Fuel recycling facility:** $1.68B
