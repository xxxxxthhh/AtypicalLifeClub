## Executive Summary

Lam Research is the etch-and-deposition dashboard for the semicap-equipment layer. If AI demand is really driving HBM, advanced DRAM, 3D structures, advanced logic, and packaging complexity, Lam should show it through system revenue, service revenue, guidance, and customer-geography mix.

For the March 2026 quarter, revenue was $5.841B, GAAP gross margin was 49.8%, GAAP operating margin was 35.0%, and GAAP diluted EPS was $1.45. Non-GAAP gross margin was 49.9%, operating margin was 35.0%, and diluted EPS was $1.47. Guidance for the June 2026 quarter was $6.60B revenue +/- $400M, 50.5% gross margin +/- 1 point, 36.5% operating margin +/- 1 point, and $1.65 diluted EPS +/- $0.15.

At the 2026-07-02 close of $351.41, LRCX's market cap was about $439.5B and EV about $438.4B. The June-quarter guide midpoint implies a $26.4B annualized revenue run-rate, or about 16.6x EV / run-rate sales. Yahoo forward EPS of about $8.03 implies roughly 43.8x forward earnings.

## 1. Business And Chain Role

Lam is not the lithography monopoly; it is the process-intensity check. Advanced nodes, HBM, 3D NAND, backside power, advanced packaging, and device complexity need more etch and deposition steps. That makes LRCX a direct triangulation point against ASML and AMAT: if ASML bookings and AMAT breadth are strong, Lam should also see a stronger process-tool mix.

The company also has a meaningful services base. March-quarter Systems revenue was $3.731B and customer-support-related and other revenue was $2.111B. That service base matters because it can validate utilization and installed-tool intensity even when new system orders are uneven.

## 2. Bull / Bear Frame

**Bull case:** management described record revenue and EPS as AI-driven demand reshapes the semiconductor industry. Guidance stepped up to $6.60B revenue, and margin guidance remained strong. Korea and Taiwan together represented 46% of revenue, which fits the HBM / leading-edge manufacturing read-through.

**Bear case:** China represented 34% of revenue in the March quarter. That is a large export-control and digestion risk. Deferred revenue was $2.22B, and shipments to Japan for future revenue recognition were about $434M; acceptance timing matters. At more than 16x annualized sales, the stock needs the AI process-intensity cycle to stay exceptional.

## 3. Key Monitoring

1. **Etch / deposition demand:** Systems revenue was $3.731B. Watch whether the next prints show AI process complexity broadening beyond one customer or memory pocket.
2. **China / Korea / Taiwan mix:** China 34%, Korea 23%, Taiwan 23% is powerful but risky. Any China restriction or local-demand digestion would matter.
3. **Deferred revenue and Japan acceptance:** deferred revenue was $2.22B and Japan future-revenue shipments were about $434M.
4. **June-quarter guidance:** $6.60B revenue and 36.5% operating margin are the immediate conversion test.
5. **Service resilience:** customer-support-related and other revenue was $2.111B. Weak service would suggest installed-base utilization is cooling.

## 4. Valuation Context

Current price-ledger input: $351.41 close on 2026-07-02, market cap about $439.5B, EV about $438.4B. Against the June-quarter guide midpoint of $6.60B, LRCX trades at about 16.6x annualized run-rate sales. Using Yahoo forward EPS of $8.03, the stock is around 43.8x forward earnings.

That multiple is demanding for a cyclical equipment company. It can be defended if etch / deposition intensity grows structurally with AI memory and advanced logic. It is vulnerable if China revenue rolls over, acceptance delays rise, or system demand proves pulled forward.

## 5. Conclusion

LRCX is the process-intensity check for P2. The report's role is to ask whether AI-driven capacity needs more than lithography: more etch, more deposition, more service, and more tool intensity per wafer. The stance is **neutral-watch** because execution is strong but valuation and China exposure are both elevated.
