## Executive Summary

ASML is the common-constraint report for the semicap-equipment layer. If AI capex is real, it eventually has to pass through lithography capacity, EUV availability, High-NA timing, customer long-term agreements, and export-control limits. ASML does not validate GPU demand by itself; it tests whether foundry and memory capex is committed deeply enough to absorb the hardest upstream bottleneck.

The official Q1 2026 print was strong: net sales EUR 8.767B, gross margin 53.0%, net income EUR 2.757B, and Q2 guidance of EUR 8.4-9.0B revenue with 51-52% gross margin. Management guided FY2026 net sales to EUR 36-40B and gross margin to 51-53%. The Q4 2025 order baseline matters even more for the chain: quarterly net bookings were EUR 13.158B, of which EUR 7.4B was EUV, and year-end backlog was EUR 38.797B.

At the 2026-07-02 ADR close of $1,769.32, ASML's market cap was about $681.9B. Using Q1 cash and short-term investments of EUR 8.376B and total debt of about EUR 2.706B, net cash was roughly EUR 5.7B, so EV is close to market cap. At EUR/USD 1.1440 and the EUR 38B FY2026 sales-guide midpoint, ASML trades at roughly 15.6x 2026E sales. That is a premium for monopoly quality, not a cheap cyclical entry point.

## 1. Business And Chain Role

ASML owns the lithography bottleneck that every leading-edge logic, HBM-related memory, and advanced-node capacity plan must face. The role is not merely "equipment supplier"; it is the capacity gatekeeper. TSMC, Samsung, Intel, SK Hynix, Micron, and advanced ASIC designers can announce capex, but EUV / High-NA delivery cadence decides how much of that capex is executable.

For the AI infrastructure map, ASML is the equipment-layer equivalent of TSMC in foundry: a common constraint that connects downstream chip demand to upstream tool availability. If ASML bookings, backlog conversion, installed-base services, and export-control disclosures stay firm, the AI capex story has physical support. If they weaken while downstream companies keep announcing capacity, the chain is running on intention rather than committed tool orders.

## 2. Bull / Bear Frame

**Bull case:** Q4 bookings and Q1 guidance suggest customers are still locking in capacity for 2026 and beyond. EUV bookings of EUR 7.4B in Q4 2025 and a EUR 38.8B backlog provide a visible conversion base. Q1 installed-base management sales of EUR 2.488B add recurring service economics, and management explicitly tied the growth outlook to AI infrastructure investments and customer capacity acceleration.

**Bear case:** the valuation already assumes scarcity remains monetizable. Export controls are a real swing factor: ASML said the FY2026 guidance range accommodates possible outcomes from ongoing export-control discussions. China exposure, High-NA adoption timing, customer digestion after a heavy booking quarter, and a 15x+ sales multiple leave little room for order disappointment.

## 3. Key Monitoring

1. **EUV / High-NA order cadence:** Q4 2025 net bookings were EUR 13.158B, including EUR 7.4B EUV. A sustained decline in EUV orders would weaken the "AI capex is committed" read-through.
2. **Backlog conversion:** year-end 2025 backlog was EUR 38.797B. The key is whether backlog converts into 2026-2027 sales without cancellations, delays, or lower-margin mix.
3. **Export-control / China path:** guidance explicitly allows for export-control outcomes. Any tighter restriction that reduces shipped systems or service access is a direct bear signal.
4. **Installed-base services:** Q1 installed-base management sales were EUR 2.488B. Service resilience helps cushion system cyclicality.
5. **Valuation tolerance:** at about 15.6x FY2026E sales, ASML needs both scarcity and growth to remain visible.

## 4. Valuation Context

Current price-ledger input: $1,769.32 ADR close on 2026-07-02, market cap about $681.9B, EUR/USD 1.1440 on 2026-07-03. With FY2026 sales guidance of EUR 36-40B, the midpoint converts to about $43.5B, putting EV / 2026E sales near 15.6x after adjusting for ASML's small net-cash position.

That valuation can be justified only if EUV / High-NA remains the binding constraint and if the EUR 38.8B backlog is high-quality. If customers delay capacity or if export rules reduce the addressable shipped base, the stock has valuation downside before the long-term lithography moat changes.

## 5. Conclusion

ASML is a high-quality, high-expectation common constraint. It should lead P2 because it defines the equipment bottleneck that AMAT, LRCX, KLAC, SNPS, and CDNS will triangulate around. The right stance is **neutral-watch**: the moat is unusually strong, but the current multiple already capitalizes a durable AI lithography shortage.
