## 1. What is COPX?

The Global X Copper Miners ETF (COPX) tracks the **Solactive Global Copper Miners Total Return Index**, providing exposure to a diversified basket of global copper mining companies. Launched in April 2010, it has grown to ~$7.4 billion in AUM, making it the largest pure-play copper miners ETF.

### Key Characteristics

- **Pure copper focus:** Unlike broader mining ETFs, COPX concentrates specifically on copper miners
- **Global diversification:** Holdings span Canada (~32-37%), Poland, Sweden, Japan, UK, US, Peru, Chile, and Australia
- **Mid-to-large cap bias:** Top holdings include major producers like Freeport-McMoRan, Southern Copper, and KGHM
- **Semi-annual rebalancing:** Index reconstituted twice yearly

### 2.2 Supply Side: Structural Constraints

#### Mine Production

- Global mine output expected to grow ~2.3% in 2026
- Refined copper output growth slowing to just 0.9% (vs. 3.4% in 2025)
- Global production projected to **peak at ~33 Mt by 2030** â€” well below projected demand

#### Key Supply Constraints

1. **Declining ore grades:** Major operations (Codelco, Escondida) seeing lower grades, higher costs
2. **17-year development cycle:** Average time from discovery to production
3. **Operational disruptions:** Grasberg mudslide, El Teniente tunnel collapse, Kamoa-Kakula flooding
4. **Concentrate shortage:** Chinese smelters planning 10% production cuts due to low treatment charges
5. **Resource nationalism:** Stricter regulations, community opposition, environmental requirements
6. **Underinvestment:** ~80 new major mines needed by 2040 to meet demand
