## Executive Summary

Applied Materials is the broad semicap-equipment dashboard. Where ASML tests the lithography bottleneck, AMAT tests whether AI-related foundry, DRAM, advanced packaging, and service demand is broad enough to lift multiple equipment categories at once.

Q2 FY2026 revenue was a record $7.91B, up 11% year over year. GAAP gross margin was 49.9%, non-GAAP gross margin was 50.0%, GAAP EPS was $3.51, and non-GAAP EPS was $2.86. Semiconductor Systems revenue was $5.965B, with foundry/logic/other at 67%, DRAM at 29%, and Flash at 4%. Management guided Q3 FY2026 revenue to $8.950B +/- $500M and non-GAAP EPS to $3.36 +/- $0.20.

At the 2026-07-02 close of $603.04, AMAT's market cap was about $478.8B and EV about $477.8B. The Q3 guide midpoint implies a $35.8B annualized revenue run-rate, or roughly 13.3x EV / run-rate sales. Yahoo forward EPS of about $16.40 implies roughly 36.8x forward earnings. The setup is strong, but the multiple already assumes the calendar-2026 equipment upcycle holds.

## 1. Business And Chain Role

AMAT is the breadth check for semicap equipment. Its product exposure spans deposition, etch, ion implant, process control-adjacent systems, services, and advanced packaging. That makes it useful for testing whether AI capex is just an EUV bottleneck or a broader wafer-fab-equipment cycle.

The Q2 mix matters: foundry / logic / other was 67% of Semiconductor Systems sales, while DRAM was 29%. If AI demand is pushing both leading-edge logic and HBM DRAM capacity, AMAT should show it through Semiconductor Systems growth, service resilience, order visibility, and supply-chain preparedness.

## 2. Bull / Bear Frame

**Bull case:** management said the semiconductor equipment business is expected to grow more than 30% in calendar 2026, driven by AI buildout, leading-edge logic, DRAM, and advanced packaging. Q3 guidance stepped up sharply from Q2 revenue, and management has increased build plans, inventory, and logistics capacity to meet demand.

**Bear case:** the same breadth creates cyclicality and geopolitical risk. China and Taiwan each represented 27% of Q2 revenue, while Korea was 20%. If export controls, customer timing, or inventory digestion slow the buildout, AMAT's wide exposure can transmit weakness quickly. The valuation is not priced for a normal equipment downcycle.

## 3. Key Monitoring

1. **Semiconductor Systems growth:** Q2 Semiconductor Systems revenue was $5.965B with 35.1% operating margin. This is the core AI capex readout.
2. **Foundry / DRAM mix:** 67% foundry / logic / other and 29% DRAM should confirm both advanced logic and HBM-related memory demand.
3. **China / Taiwan exposure:** Q2 geography was Taiwan 27%, China 27%, Korea 20%. Export-control and customer concentration risk remain material.
4. **Q3 conversion:** the Q3 midpoint is $8.95B revenue and $3.36 non-GAAP EPS. Missing that would challenge the "upcycle in full force" claim.
5. **Supply-chain readiness:** management increased build plan, inventory, and logistics capacity. That is bullish if orders convert, bearish if demand slips.

## 4. Valuation Context

Current price-ledger input: $603.04 close on 2026-07-02, market cap about $478.8B, EV about $477.8B. Against the Q3 FY2026 revenue-guide midpoint of $8.95B, AMAT trades around 13.3x annualized run-rate sales. Using Yahoo forward EPS of $16.40, the stock is around 36.8x forward earnings.

This is a quality-cycle valuation. The stock can work if calendar-2026 equipment growth does exceed 30% and if foundry plus DRAM demand both confirm. It becomes vulnerable if revenue is pulled forward, if China rules tighten, or if the broad equipment cycle cools before AI capacity has fully converted.

## 5. Conclusion

AMAT is the semicap breadth dashboard. It is less monopolistic than ASML, but more diversified across the tool stack. The right stance is **neutral-watch**: the business is executing well, but the valuation requires a sustained broad equipment upcycle rather than a single strong quarter.
