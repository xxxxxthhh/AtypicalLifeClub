## 1. Business And Chain Role

Digital Realty is a global data-center REIT offering colocation, interconnection, hyperscale and development capacity. For the AI-infrastructure chain, its core question is whether customers are willing to sign long-term leases and convert demand into physical rooms, network interconnection and powered capacity.

| 0-1MW + interconnection | $98M bookings, +42% YoY | Demand is not only the largest single-tenant hyperscale deals |

## 2. Bull / Bear Framework

Bull case:

1. **Leases turn AI demand into cash flow.** $707M bookings and $1.8B backlog are closer to deployment than chip-order headlines.
2. **0-1MW plus interconnection growth broadens the signal.** $98M bookings and +42% YoY growth cross-check enterprise, network and cloud-platform demand.
3. **The global platform and roughly 9GW capacity provide runway.** More than 55 metros, 300+ data centers and future development capacity can support hyperscaler and AI workloads.
4. **Core FFO guidance moved higher.** 2026 Core FFO/share guidance of $8.00-$8.10 indicates continued support from pricing and same-capital NOI.

Bear case:

1. **Backlog is not immediate revenue.** New leases had a 19-month weighted-average lag to contractual commencement in Q1, so deployment can trail upstream orders.
2. **Power and permitting remain bottlenecks.** DLR's >6GW future development capacity only matters if power, land, equipment and construction line up.
3. **Leverage and cost of capital are heavy.** About $15.6B net debt means development requires continuous access to debt and equity capital.
4. **Valuation already includes a data-center scarcity premium.** Around 21x 2026 Core FFO guidance and about 12x EV/TTM revenue require backlog conversion to stay strong.

## 5. Conclusion

DLR is a necessary check on AI data-center leasing and interconnection. Upstream chip, optical, power equipment and neocloud reports all describe buildout demand; DLR tests whether that demand lands in rooms, leases, cross-connects and future capacity.

The current view is **neutral watch / data-center facility dashboard**. Bookings and backlog are strong, but the stock already carries a data-center scarcity premium. The key is to track backlog commencement, 0-1MW plus interconnection, development power and cost of capital to decide whether AI demand is becoming long-term lease cash flow or whether upstream orders are running too far ahead of physical deployment.
