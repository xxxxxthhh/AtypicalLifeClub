## Executive Summary

Cadence is the EDA/IP dashboard paired with Synopsys. It validates whether AI demand is visible in design automation, IP, simulation, backlog, recurring revenue visibility, and agentic design workflows. If SNPS is the EDA/IP common constraint after Ansys, CDNS is the cleaner backlog and execution readout.

Q1 2026 revenue was $1.474B versus $1.242B in Q1 2025. GAAP operating margin was 29.3%, non-GAAP operating margin was 44.7%, GAAP EPS was $1.23, and non-GAAP EPS was $1.96. Quarter-end backlog was $8.0B, and revenue expected to be recognized in the next 12 months from remaining performance obligations was $4.0B. Management guided FY2026 revenue to $6.125-6.225B and non-GAAP EPS to $7.85-7.95.

At the 2026-07-02 close of $373.14, CDNS had about $102.9B market cap and $104.5B EV. Against the $6.175B FY2026 revenue midpoint, EV / sales is about 16.9x. Against the $7.90 non-GAAP EPS midpoint, P/E is about 47.2x.

## 1. Business And Chain Role

CDNS sits at the design-complexity layer. Its signals are different from tool vendors: backlog, RPO conversion, IP growth, system design and analysis growth, AI-assisted design workflows, and recurring design software demand. That makes it a useful cross-check against both SNPS and custom-silicon reports such as Broadcom, Marvell, AMD, and NVIDIA.

The company disclosed IP business growth of 22% year over year, driven by AI infrastructure, HPC, and automotive, with HBM, LPDDR, PCIe, SerDes, and foundation IP adoption. System Design and Analysis revenue grew 18% year over year, and the Hexagon D&E acquisition adds structural and multibody dynamics exposure.

## 2. Bull / Bear Frame

**Bull case:** backlog of $8.0B and $4.0B of next-12-month RPO create strong visibility. Non-GAAP operating margin of 44.7% shows high software economics. Management framed AI demand, AgentStack, ChipStack, ViraStack, and InnoStack Super Agents as expanding the design-flow opportunity.

**Bear case:** the valuation is high even for a high-quality EDA compounder. At nearly 17x FY2026E sales and more than 47x non-GAAP EPS guidance, CDNS must convert backlog without margin slippage. Hexagon integration, agentic-AI monetization, export controls, and any slowdown in custom AI chip starts are the key risks.

## 3. Key Monitoring

1. **Backlog / RPO conversion:** backlog was $8.0B and next-12-month RPO was $4.0B. Conversion should support 2026 guidance.
2. **Agentic design workflow:** AgentStack and the broader AI-assisted design stack must move from product narrative to measurable adoption.
3. **IP growth:** IP grew 22% year over year, driven by AI infrastructure, HPC, and automotive. HBM / SerDes / PCIe pull-through is the tell.
4. **Hexagon / Physical AI integration:** the acquisition expands system simulation exposure but adds integration risk.
5. **Valuation discipline:** at about 47x FY2026E non-GAAP EPS, small disappointments can matter.

## 4. Valuation Context

Current price-ledger input: $373.14 close on 2026-07-02, market cap about $102.9B, EV about $104.5B. FY2026 revenue midpoint is $6.175B and non-GAAP EPS midpoint is $7.90, implying about 16.9x EV / sales and about 47.2x non-GAAP P/E.

This is a premium execution multiple. The premium can persist if backlog, IP, and AI design-flow adoption compound together. It becomes fragile if growth is mainly backlog harvesting, if Hexagon integration dilutes margin, or if AI chip design starts slow.

## 5. Conclusion

CDNS is the clean EDA/IP dashboard. It complements SNPS by providing a sharper backlog, RPO, IP, and design-flow readout. The stance is **neutral-watch**: the business quality is high, but the valuation requires very clean execution.
