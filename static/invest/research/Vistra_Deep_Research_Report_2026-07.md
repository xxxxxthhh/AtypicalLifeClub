## 1. Business And Chain Role

Vistra is an integrated generation and retail power platform with nuclear, gas, coal / transition assets, and retail power capabilities. AI data centers do not need only "green power"; they need dispatchable capacity that can come online quickly and match load volatility. VST therefore carries more merchant power and gas-capacity beta than CEG.

The chain role is to prevent the power layer from becoming a single clean-nuclear story. If real data-center growth requires a mixed stack, VST should show it through Meta nuclear agreements, Cogentrix gas capacity, PJM/Texas capacity prices, and cash-flow support for leverage. If VST weakens while CEG holds, the power thesis may be real but narrower and cleaner than the market's broad trade.

## 5. Bull Case

The bull case is that AI data centers need both clean baseload and dispatchable capacity. VST owns both, and the Meta agreements validate direct hyperscaler procurement from existing nuclear assets. Cogentrix adds about 5,500MW of gas generation, which can become more valuable if PJM/Texas capacity markets continue to price data-center load growth.

VST also has higher equity beta than CEG. If CEG anchors the quality trade and NRG proves broader merchant/retail scarcity, VST can sit in the middle: more contracted than NRG, more levered and capacity-sensitive than CEG. A strong bull read requires Meta execution, Cogentrix close, firm capacity prices, and FCF that covers leverage rather than merely funding expansion.

## 6. Bear Case

The bear case is that VST is a power-scarcity trade with too much leverage and too much commodity/regulatory exposure. Q1 net debt was about $19.3B, and acquisitions plus capex can absorb cash quickly. Even with TTM FCF about $1.80B, the equity has less room for error if gas prices, capacity prices, or regulatory decisions move against the company.

The PPA details also remain important. Meta's involvement validates the category, but pricing, cost allocation, uprate timing, and regulatory approval can determine whether the economics match the market's expectations. If AI capex or data-center growth expectations cool, VST can de-rate faster than CEG because it is the higher-beta expression of the same power theme.

### Conclusion And Review

Vistra is the high-beta comparison to Constellation. CEG is the steadier nuclear-led common-constraint anchor; VST is the nuclear plus gas plus merchant-capacity dashboard. Covering it prevents the power layer from becoming only a clean nuclear story when real data centers may need a mixed power stack.

VST's chain-validation job is to test whether AI power demand spreads from clean baseload PPAs into dispatchable gas capacity, PJM/Texas capacity value, and the repricing of existing generation assets. It is higher beta than CEG and more pure-power than NRG; that lets it validate power scarcity faster, but also makes it reverse faster when gas, regulation, capacity prices, or leverage move the wrong way.

The expectation gap is mildly negative: at $151.05, about 3.6x EV/TTM sales, about 23x TTM GAAP P/E, and about $19.3B net debt, the market already prices Meta nuclear PPAs, Cogentrix gas capacity, and PJM/Texas capacity scarcity; our base case recognizes real upside but does not ignore the faster downside from leverage and merchant exposure.

The current stance is **cautious, medium conviction**. The 30% bull / 40% base / 30% bear grid says the upside requires Meta PPA, Cogentrix, capacity pricing, and FCF to all deliver; the bear path only needs one or two of those variables to move against the thesis. Cautious is not a bearish call on the mixed power stack. It is the judgment that at the current price, high beta's downside speed is slightly greater than the certainty of upside.

Upgrade trigger: Move to neutral-watch or constructive if Meta PPA capacity / price / uprate path becomes clearer, Cogentrix closes on time and converts about 5,500MW of gas capacity into data-center or capacity-market earnings, PJM/Texas capacity prices keep rising, and FCF supports deleveraging. Downgrade trigger: Move to bearish-avoid if Cogentrix is delayed or synergies miss, Meta PPA economics disappoint, PJM/Texas capacity premiums fade, or net-debt / FCF pressure rises from capex, buybacks, fuel, or regulation.
