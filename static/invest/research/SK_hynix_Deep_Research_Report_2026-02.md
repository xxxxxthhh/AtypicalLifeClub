## 1. Company Overview

SK hynix is the world's second-largest memory chipmaker (behind Samsung Electronics) and the dominant leader in HBM. Founded in 1983, headquartered in Icheon, South Korea. Parent company SK Square holds ~20% equity.

### Business Mix

| Segment | Revenue Share | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| **DRAM** | ~73% | Including HBM, DDR5, LPDDR5 |
| **NAND** | ~27% | Rose to global #2 after acquiring Intel's NAND business in 2021 |

### HBM: The Profit Engine

HBM is high-performance memory designed for AI accelerators (GPUs/TPUs), achieving extreme bandwidth through vertical stacking of DRAM dies. SK hynix is the pioneer and leader of this category.

- HBM ASP is 5-6x conventional DRAM
- HBM margins estimated at 60-70%+
- A rising share of DRAM revenue and now the core profit driver; in Q1 2026 DRAM ASP rose ~60% (mid-range) QoQ, pushing the overall operating margin to 72%

## 2. Industry Context: AI-Driven Memory Supercycle

### 2.1 Why HBM Is Irreplaceable

AI model training and inference require enormous memory bandwidth, which conventional DDR cannot supply:

| Metric | DDR5 | HBM3e | HBM4 |
|--------|------|-------|------|
| Bandwidth | ~50 GB/s | ~1.2 TB/s | ~2.8 TB/s |
| Stack Layers | 1 | 12 | 16 |
| Interface Width | 64-bit | 1024-bit | 2048-bit |
| Main Use | PC/server | AI GPU | Next-gen AI GPU |

### 2.2 Demand Explosion

- Each NVIDIA Blackwell GPU requires 8 HBM3e stacks
- NVIDIA Rubin (2026-2027) will use HBM4
- Global AI infrastructure capex exceeds $300B in 2026
- HBM market: $16B (2024) → $40B+ (2026E) → $70B+ (2028E)
- On the Q1 2026 call, SK hynix said customer demand for the next three years already exceeds its supply capacity

### 2.3 Supply Landscape

| Company | HBM Share | Status |
|---------|----------|--------|
| SK hynix | 53% | Leader, NVIDIA's primary supplier |
| Samsung | ~35% | Catching up, HBM3e yield improving |
| Micron | ~12% | Smallest share, technology catch-up |

The three together control 100% of HBM capacity; SK hynix + Samsung control 90%.

## 4. HBM Technology Roadmap & Competition

### 4.1 Technology Evolution

| Generation | Stack | Bandwidth | Mass Production | SK hynix Position |
|-----------|-------|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| HBM3 | 8-layer | 819 GB/s | 2023 | Leader |
| HBM3e | 12-layer | 1.2 TB/s | 2024-2025 | NVIDIA exclusive/primary |
| HBM4 | 12-16 layer | 2.8 TB/s | 2026 H2 | Competition intensifying |
| HBM4e | 16-layer+ | TBD | 2027 (mass-production target) | Samples in H2 2026 |

### 4.2 HBM4: The Competitive Inflection

HBM4 is the key inflection point:
- All three (SK hynix, Samsung, Micron) have submitted 16-Hi HBM4 samples to NVIDIA
- Samsung reports 11.7 Gbps/pin test speed (SK hynix targets 10 Gbps)
- NVIDIA Rubin GPU will be the first HBM4 customer
- SK hynix plans to ship 7th-gen HBM4E samples in H2 and reach mass production in 2027
- **SK hynix's exclusive advantage may narrow in the HBM4 era**

### 4.3 Moat Assessment

**Strong:**
- Advanced packaging (TSV, hybrid bonding) 12-18 month lead
- Deep NVIDIA co-development relationship
- Yield advantage driving cost leadership
- 2026 capacity sold out; demand exceeds supply for the next three years

**Weakening:**
- Samsung HBM4 catch-up accelerating
- Micron gaining U.S. policy support
- HBM4 architecture changes may reset the competitive landscape

## 7. Investment Thesis: Bull vs. Bear

### 🐂 Bull Case

**1. HBM monopoly (high conviction)**
53% market share + NVIDIA lock-in; the company says demand exceeds supply for the next three years. This isn't ordinary leadership but near-monopoly with exceptional demand visibility.

**2. Earnings scale and net cash (high conviction)**
Q1 2026 alone produced ₩37.6T operating profit and ₩40.3T net profit, with ₩35T net cash. Even after the re-rating, the sheer scale of earnings and cash generation cushions the downside; FY2026 earnings will far exceed FY2025.

**3. AI capex supercycle (high conviction)**
NVIDIA Blackwell/Rubin require enormous HBM. Microsoft/Amazon/Meta/Google combined capex exceeds $300B in 2026. HBM is the highest-certainty AI beneficiary.

**4. Technology lead (moderate-high conviction)**
A 12-18 month advanced-packaging yield lead over Samsung; deep NVIDIA co-development; HBM4E samples in H2 2026 and mass production in 2027 — a clear roadmap.

**5. Shareholder returns and governance (moderate conviction)**
The company has committed to higher returns; record FY2025 dividends. Korea's "Value-up" reform is improving governance and narrowing the Korea discount.

### 🐻 Bear Case

**1. Memory cyclicality + a re-rated valuation (high conviction, weight rising)**
Memory cycles every 3-4 years. FY2023 was a massive loss (-₩7.7T); Q1 2026 operating margin is already 72% — almost certainly a cyclical peak. Unlike February, the stock is up ~3.5x at P/S ~16x, so valuation no longer provides a cushion; peak margins + peak valuation together make mean reversion the largest downside risk.

**2. HBM4 competition (moderate-high conviction)**
Samsung is catching up in HBM4, even reporting faster test speeds. The exclusive premium may shrink in 2026-2027.

**3. HBM pricing pressure (moderate conviction)**
With all three expanding HBM4 capacity in H2 2026, added supply could cut prices 10-15%, directly hitting the 72% peak margin.

**4. Customer concentration (moderate conviction)**
Heavy NVIDIA dependence. What if NVIDIA moves toward in-house memory or sharply diversifies suppliers?

**5. Korea Discount (fact)**
Korea carries a structural valuation discount — chaebol governance, weak minority protections, geopolitical risk. It has narrowed with fundamentals but remains.

**6. Geopolitics (moderate conviction)**
Korean semiconductors are caught in US-China tensions; U.S. export controls on China could spill into Korean supply chains.

### Bottom Line

SK hynix is one of the AI era's most essential "picks and shovels" — if NVIDIA is AI's brain, SK hynix is AI's memory. A single quarter of ₩37.6T operating profit, a 72% margin, and ₩35T net cash prove an extraordinarily powerful earnings machine.

But the nature of the opportunity has changed since February. Back then the core pitch was "a single-digit forward P/E, deeply undervalued." Now the stock is up ~3.5x, market cap has crossed $1.3T, and P/S is ~16x — the deep-value gap has closed. What remains is not "buying it cheap" but judging "how long the peak lasts" — a 72% margin is almost certainly not sustainable through the cycle.

**Risk-matching characteristics (descriptive, not investment advice):**
- Cycle position: currently in the high-boom phase of the memory supercycle, with peak margins and peak valuation together — highly sensitive to the cycle turning
- Valuation: the deep-value window has closed; this is now closer to "owning a high-quality monopolist at a full-ish price," calling for a risk budget that can absorb cyclical swings
- Access: Korean-listed; U.S. investors go via EWY/Interactive Brokers or hold directly, or use Micron (MU) as the closest proxy
- Governance and geopolitics: the chaebol discount and US-China tensions are structural backdrops to factor in beforehand

**Key watch point:** 2026 Q2 and H2 are the critical windows — whether peak margins hold, whether ASPs come under pressure as HBM4 ramps, and whether Samsung regains share. Fundamental top signals matter more than valuation here: at a cyclical peak, whether the stock looks cheap matters far less than how much longer earnings can keep climbing.
